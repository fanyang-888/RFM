# Phase 0 - Hiring Manager Audit (US Entry-Level Data/AI, 2026)

## 10-15 Second First Impression

This looks like a student analytics repository that has some real work in it, but it does not yet look like an interview-ready portfolio project.

## Current Hiring Signal

Current signal: **early learning-stage data analyst / junior ML learner**.  
Why: the repo includes notebooks and clustering work, but business framing, reproducibility, and decision quality are not presented at professional depth.

## What Is Weak or Low-Signal

1. **README is too light for hiring impact**  
   It lists files and setup, but does not present a strong business narrative, clear objective, or decision-oriented outcome.

2. **Business value is generic**  
   The project claims personalization and targeting value, but does not tie analysis to practical segment actions, risks, or prioritization logic.

3. **Method choice is under-justified**  
   KMeans/DBSCAN are used, but there is not enough discussion of why these methods are appropriate versus alternatives.

4. **Evaluation is metric-heavy but decision-light**  
   Metrics exist (SSE/silhouette), but there is little interpretation of what metrics imply for product or marketing decisions.

5. **Repository story is fragmented**  
   There is overlap between notebooks, overview docs, and scripts. A recruiter scanning quickly cannot see one clear "start here -> approach -> results -> recommendation" path.

6. **Interview readiness is weak**  
   There is no section that shows trade-offs, assumptions, failure modes, or "what I would do next in production."

7. **Technical maturity signals are incomplete**  
   Good start with modular `src/`, but no explicit data quality checks summary, no reproducibility contract, and no concise explanation of engineering decisions.

## Top Reasons a Recruiter Might Lose Interest

1. "I still don't know the exact business problem in one sentence."
2. "I can't quickly see what was learned and what action a business should take."
3. "This feels like notebook exploration, not a scoped business case."
4. "Method and metric choices are not defended like an analyst/ML practitioner in a real team."
5. "I cannot judge candidate decision quality from the repository narrative."

## What Needs to Improve Next

- Strong business framing and project positioning.
- Professional README structure for executive scanning.
- Methodology justification and trade-off discussion.
- Clear hiring signals: decision-making, rigor, communication, and product thinking.
